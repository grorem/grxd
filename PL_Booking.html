PRIVATE LESSON BOOKING.

THE GOAL;

REDUCE REQUIRED* CLICKS TO INCREASE LESSON BOOKINGS.

We had an old private lesson booking system with and a new, refactored system coming online. The business asked us to increase private lesson booking rates. As quantitative data showed Private Lesson bookings as significantly lower than that of Group lessons. A metric closely tied to student satisfaction scores.

We put a banner in the online school which students followed to both a survey and an asynchronous, unmoderated click-through prototype session using maze.design.

The feedback we collected suggested something causing a high abandon rate within the Private Lesson booking journey.

Insights from the survey and unmoderated sessions revealed a few common threads:

“I don’t know what to do”
“I don’t have time for this”
“I can’t find a teacher”
“I have to do this all again?”

A NEW BOOKING FLOW.

Using insights from the user surveys and unmoderated testing, as well as alignment meetings with stakeholders, we came up with a set of user goals for us to guide us:

1. Reduce friction & remove repetition.

2. Separate new & recurring booking flows

3. Always accessible & available.

From this, I put together the wireframes and flows you see on the right.

PROTOTYPE EARLY, ITERATE QUICKLY.

Leveraging our recently made and still forming brand design system & guidelines, I put together prototypes for mobile and web, so we could get a better feel for our designs, and I wanted to put a phone right in stakeholders’ hands at the next review when we aimed to additionally gain buy-in for moderated studies using the prototypes to better inform final design efforts.

TESTING.

Selection criteria
Five existing students from the France and Spain markets currently studying in the first 3 of 16 levels of EF English Live, with 2 Free private lessons given as incentive.

Materials
A session guide (script) & a high-fidelity prototype of the mobile experience.

Method
Remote task-based moderated session with a facilitator fluent in the local language and an observer/note-taker.

Tasks included: Booking a lesson, rebooking a previous teacher for next Friday, finding a teacher with a specific knowledge background, filtering by teachers fluent in your language, requesting a topic not available in the list shown.

OUTCOME & IMPACT;

AN INCREASE IN RELEVANT BOOKINGS

What did I change from the feedback gathered in user studies?

Pre-select class next-most not yet done lesson topic rather than highlighting with a star, as it wasn’t clear what the star meant.

Make teacher profiles/preferences more immediately relevant to classes and actionable: e.g. add toggle ‘show teachers who speak my language’ instead of the need to browse teachers by language.

Instead of just showing a previously selected teacher as unavailable, after time selection, add toggle to show previous teacher availability. Let the student decide if they want to prioritize booking time or the teacher.

We could immediately see the difference, with the new system at first rolled out to a smaller set of students, the abandon rate had taken a nose dive as could be continuously compared to students still on the old system.

This update has since been rolled out to virtually all students by merit of their products having been migrated to the new system.
