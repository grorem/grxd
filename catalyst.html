THE LESSON PLAYER

A new learning platform, data visuals, reimagined learning activities and new authoring tools.

THE PROBLEM SPACE.
LESSONS, AUTHORING, DATA, TEACHERS.

A core feature of an EF student’s learning journey is self-study: Learning activities in context of a course cirriculum alongside group and private classes with teachers.

More than simply align the experience between mobile and web [they weren't at all aligned], We set out to update the underlying technology that serves this content and learning to students, to update the experience to be less an arbitrary march of ticking boxes (lessons, classes, activities), and more an informative, engaging and personalized learning journey.

in doing so our first step was to refresh the design of the legacy self study content and activities, interactions and experience of what we have today, as well as set up first steps towards a vision of the product towards the future.
This future vision being a far more connected experience, where the tools interact with other core features and moments of the student journey.

FLEXIBILITY, SCALABILITY & A DESIGN SYSTEM.

With the dev team working in Angular, and an existing global design system, which places emphasis on core [base rules and components] and expression [where we change certain attributes to align with a product's particular needs].

We not only wanted to bring legacy design into this new modern system, but set up the "expression" that our product would take in relation to our other experiences and apps across the wider EF.STUDIO product.
