Project Phoenix.

a refreshed product, a new story

"We all want to be cool, but nobody wants to be THAT cool."

This remains my favorite quote of user feedback to date. Once we had mock ups completed, I decided to check the the overall look and feed, with particular attention to the images, with 30 people. and one of them said "asdf".

This project was part web design, part strategy, and part base product experience.

We needed a web experience, but one not just to sell an updated product, but one that can optimize the lead-acquisition funnels by designing things modularly, so they can be readily and quickly changed depending on quantitative data we had coming in.

The concept.

Of course we needed to provide info on the updated product, yet we wanted to provide a glimpse into the offerings of EF, through experience.

This was going to rely on imagery and interactive experiences.

Testing along the way.

I tested what we had so far with 30 different people, since we wanted to be giving off the right feel for our target audiences.

"Everyone wants to be cool, not nobody wants to be THAT cool." comes from one such person calling out how the look and feel was nice but a bit unrelatable.

We opted to use many more photos from around EF, and around the world even, by connecting with a staff photographer friend of ours who was traveling for imagery already for another business unit. On subsequent tests, it was clear that this helped the messaging to be far more relatable to people, yet of high quality.
